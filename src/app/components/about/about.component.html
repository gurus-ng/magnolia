About us

We are a community that exists to inspire growth and make the path to finding one’s self as a young adult easier by providing the necessary resources such as mentorships,mental health workshops and internships.

Our mission is to make youth life easier.

We started off with just the Open Talk Sessions in 2018 and considering the growth and expansion happening with new projects there’s been the need to rebrand and position under a big umbrella hence, the new name THE MAGNOLIA COMMUNITY.

Angela
Wereko-Anderson

Director of Strategy

Angela Wereko-Anderson has produced and consulted within the arts and culture sector in the UK for over five years. Having worked with galleries such as Tate and Science Gallery London, she has a vested interest in initiatives that engage and encourage collaboration and exchange through connecting schools, galleries, artists and cultural institutions worldwide. She believes that everyone has a creative spirit that once ignited, can aid them in transforming themselves and the society they live in.

She also champions diversity and believes that everyone should have access to opportunities, regardless of their race, gender or socio-economic background. Her experience also ranges across various industries-banking, finance and performing arts to name but a few.

Born and bred in Ghana for a short time before moving to the UK, she aims to bring her experience back home, by creating platforms and access to initiatives that bring the very best out of young Ghanaians in Ghana.

Magnolia Board
Joseph Maafo Budu

Board Chair

Mr Joseph Maafo Budu is a native of Tutu Akuapem, in the eastern region of Ghana. born on 6th June 1953.He holds a BA degree in economics and statistics from the university of Ghana (1976) and an MA in educational administration and statistics from institute of education, university of London (1986).

His professional career was in university administration, having served at: University of Ghana from 1977 to 2013, Akrofi christaller Institute in akropong from 2003 to 2008 and Ghana Institute of Management and Public Administration from 2013 to 2016. He is an active Christian, worshipping at Legon Interdenominational Church and Tutu methodist church.
